## 3. Glossary of Key Terms

This glossary supports shared understanding across teams and organisations.  
Terms are contextualised for ethical, strategic, and creative AI use.

---

### A

**AI Awareness**  
Understanding how AI systems perceive, reason, and generate outputs — including assumptions, limitations, and confidence markers.

**AI Capability**  
A developmental construct combining awareness, co-agency, innovation, ethics, governance, and reflective practice.

**AI-Enabled Workflow**  
A human–machine process where roles, oversight, and decision points are intentional, transparent, and documented.

---

### C

**Co-Agency**  
A partnership between humans and AI systems in which responsibility, tasks, and oversight are purposefully distributed.

**Capability Ecology**  
The integrated environment of people, tools, data, and values in which AI is used — emphasising system-wide learning and relationships.

**Continuous Renewal**  
The reflective adaptation cycle that ensures AI-enabled practice evolves with technology, context, risks, and experience.

---

### D

**Design Thinking**  
An iterative framework for needs assessment, ideation, prototyping, and reflection that underpins the Capability Framework.

**Decision Audit Trail**  
A documented record of AI-influenced decisions, including reasoning, oversight, and conditions for approval.

---

### E

**Equity Audit**  
A structured assessment of who benefits and who may be harmed or excluded by an AI-enabled process or decision.

**Ethical Foresight**  
The practice of anticipating long-term social, cultural, and organisational impacts.

---

### G

**Governance Protocol**  
A formal process to evaluate, approve, monitor, and review AI adoption or use.

**Generative Practice**  
Creative, responsible use of AI to prototype, test ideas, synthesise content, or explore new possibilities.

---

### H

**Human Oversight**  
The necessary review, interpretation, and accountability steps in any AI-influenced decision.

---

### P

**Prompt Design**  
The intentional structuring of queries, constraints, examples, or instructions to guide AI behaviour.

**Participatory Governance**  
Involving diverse stakeholders in oversight and decision-making for AI systems.

---

### R

**Reflection Cycle**  
A structured process of reviewing outcomes, identifying lessons, and adjusting workflows to sustain improvement.

**Risk Surface**  
The range of ethical, operational, legal, and equity-related risks associated with an AI workflow.

---

### S

**Scenario Modelling**  
The use of AI to simulate possible futures, outcomes, or decisions, supporting strategic planning and preparation.

**Self-Assessment**  
A reflective process for identifying one’s current capability level across the six domains.
